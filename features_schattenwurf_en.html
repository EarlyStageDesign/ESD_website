Shadow diagram

This is an example of portfolio detail

In some cases, for example with high-rise buildings, the calculation the casted shadow from the high-rise is legally required in residential areas. In Early-Stage Design (ESD) this calculation is integrated into the geometry analysis. According to distinct cantonal building laws, the shadow cast by newly planned high-rise buildings must not significantly affect surrounding residential zones. Some cantons require a shadow diagram (graph-ical representation of the shadow cast) of the building at the time of the building permit, proofing that neighbor's buildings are not shadowed for more than a couple of hours, 2h-3h, on a winter day. In the context of ESD, the shadow cast by the building is calculated automatically according to its geometry, position in the globe and a specific middle winter day. An additional existent feature in ESD is the maximum buildable volume. Based on the neighboring build-ings, a volume in the interest plot can be derived, identifying on which areas a high-rise building can be placed without further issues.
